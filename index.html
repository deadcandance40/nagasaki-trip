🏺 Imari Porcelain Village

Okawachiyama · "Village of Secret Kilns"

🏺 Imari ware was first produced in 1616 when Korean potter Yi Sam-pyeong discovered kaolin in Arita.

🇳🇱 Exported to Europe via the Dutch East India Company at Dejima.

👑 The Nabeshima Clan directly controlled production, building the village in secret!

🎨 Three main styles: Ko-Imari (blue & white), Kakiemon (colorful), and Nabeshima (red & blue).

🌍 European kilns like Meissen and Royal Crown Derby later copied Imari designs.

🏮 Nagasaki Specialties

Shikairou

🍜 Original Champon (1899)

¥1,000-2,000

The birthplace of champon! Created by Chan Ping Shun for Chinese students. 2F has a Champon Museum.

🏛️ Founded in 1899 during the Meiji era. The original recipe was created to provide cheap, nutritious meals for Chinese exchange students in Nagasaki.

🥢 The name "champon" comes from the Fujian word for "mixture" — reflecting the many ingredients.

🍳 The noodles use touaku (sodium carbonate) instead of regular lye water, giving a unique chewy texture.

Fukusaya

🍰 Castella since 1624

¥1,000-2,500

Nagasaki's most famous castella shop. Uses the "betsudate" separate-whisking method. Look for the zarame sugar crystals at the bottom!

🇵🇹 Brought by Portuguese missionaries in the 16th century. The name comes from the Kingdom of Castile in Spain.

🎂 Originally called "Pão de Ló" in Portugal. The Japanese version evolved without butter, using eggs, sugar, and flour.

👑 Served to the Tokugawa shogunate and to Commodore Perry in 1854!

🍫 Try variations: matcha, chocolate, honey, and the classic with zarame (coarse sugar crystals).

♨️ Onsen & Recovery

Obama Onsen

🔥 105°C — Japan's Hottest Spring!

~45min drive

Seaside sunset views over Tachibana Bay. 1,300+ year history, mentioned in the Nara period (710-794).

🦶 Hot Foot 105 — Japan's longest foot bath (105m!)

🥚 Free steam cookers — cook eggs, veggies, seafood in hot spring steam

🍜 Try Obama Champon — a unique local twist on Nagasaki's signature dish

🎫 Yumeguri-fuda pass (¥1,300) — visit 2 hotel baths + 2 public bathhouses

📜 Described in the Hizen Fudoki (ancient report compiled in 713 AD).

🌡️ Source temperature: 105°C — one of the highest in Japan. 15,000 tons/day gush from ~30 springs.

🧂 Chloride spring — salt coats the skin, preventing dryness and keeping you warm after bathing.

🌅 Located on the west-facing coast — famous for breathtaking sunsets over Tachibana Bay.

🏥 Used as a therapeutic bath since 1914 for rheumatism, neuralgia, and skin diseases.

Unzen Onsen

🌋 "Unzen Hell" — Sulfur Vents

~1h20min drive

Japan's first national park (1934). Cool mountain air, sulfur baths, historic Christian martyrdom site.

🔥 Unzen Jigoku — steaming sulfur vents, bubbling mud pits

🥚 Onsen tamago — soft-boiled eggs cooked in the hells

🍋 Try Unzen Lemonade — local specialty

🏛️ Yunosato Public Bath — 100+ year history, ¥200 entry

🏔️ Located at 700m altitude within Unzen-Amakusa National Park, Japan's first national park (est. 1934).

🌡️ Spring quality: Highly acidic sulfur spring — strong bactericidal effect, good for skin diseases.

✝️ Between 1627-1632, many Christians were martyred here during the persecution.

🌋 Mount Unzen erupted 1990-1995, creating Heisei-shinzan (1,486m) — Nagasaki's highest peak.

🏨 Once a summer resort for Westerners from Shanghai and Hong Kong.

💀 The 1792 eruption caused a landslide tsunami killing ~15,000 people — Japan's worst volcanic disaster.

🌃 Night Views

Mount Inasa Observatory

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ One of Japan's Top 3 Night Views

"10 Million Dollar Night View" — 360° panoramic deck, rooftop observatory, café ITADAKI.

🚡 Ropeway ¥1,900 RT🚗 Car/taxi ~15min

🌃 Certified as one of the "New Three Great Night Views of the World" in 2012 alongside Monaco and Hong Kong.

🏔️ The other two Japanese night views: Mount Hakodate (Hokkaido) and Mount Rokko (Kobe).

💡 Called the "10 Million Dollar Night View" (1000万ドルの夜景) — the city lights look like a spilled jewelry box!

🚡 Ropeway runs 9:00-22:00, every 15-20 min. Free shuttle from select hotels in evenings.

🍽️ Summit restaurant ITADAKI offers dinner with the view.

🍙 The Nagasaki "Shime" (Closing Ritual)

Kaniya

Shianbashi · Since 1965

End your night like a local! Omusubi (rice ball) bar. Order the Salted Mackerel (Saba) and Minced Beef (Gyu Soboro) with signature red miso soup — considered the best hangover cure in the prefecture!

🏮 Shianbashi was Nagasaki's red light district in the 1600s. The name "Shianbashi" means "Bridge of Careful Thought."

🌳 At the fork by Yanagi Kouji street, a weeping willow tree and stone bridge railing remain — the Mikaeri Yanagi (Looking Back Willow), where men would stand wistfully looking back at their companions.

🎭 Doza-machi and Shianbashi are now a maze of narrow alleys with izakayas, cocktail bars, and hidden gems — Nagasaki's best nightlife area.
